2. What problems can tourism bring to a city

Tourism may lead to the closure of local small businesses, and it becomes more difficult for tourist to experience authentic cultures. Some landlords often increase rents when there is increased patronage and revenue. Sham Shui Po is a popular tourist area in Hong Kong. The landlords increase the rents every year. An 80-year-old history Dai Pai Dong in Sham Shui Po, popular by their delectable cuisine, was closed because they can no longer afford their rent. The burden of high rent forced them to increase prices to maintain profitability and relocate to less desirable areas. It leads to a loss of established customer bases and brand visibility. As a result, the eatery found it difficult to survive. The increase in rents due to the tourism may deter the traditional businesses continue their business. Some authentic cultures in a city may face extinction because of their closure.
